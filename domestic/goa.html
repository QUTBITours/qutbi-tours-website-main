Best time to visit Goa

  • Nov – Feb: Peak season. Perfect weather, all shacks open, Christmas & NYE crowds.
  • Mar – May: Warm but affordable. Off-season 40-50% cheaper. Beaches emptier.
  • Jun – Sep: Monsoon. Lush green, cheap, but rough seas — no water sports, some shacks closed.
  • Oct: Shoulder. Weather clears, prices still off-season.

Family / honeymoon / group tips

  • Honeymoon couples: Choose Cavelossim/Varca in South. 5-star resorts, private-beach access.
  • Families with kids: Baga/Candolim beach shacks are convenient. Book pools with kids' section.
  • Groups of friends: Anjuna, Vagator, Morjim for hostels and villa stays.
  • Seniors: South Goa resorts with easy beach access.

FAQs

Is monsoon Goa worth it?
Yes — for lush landscapes, empty beaches, and 50% cheaper hotels. Not for water sports or beach shacks.
North or South Goa for first-timers?
North for classic Goa experience (beaches, shacks, nightlife). South if you want a quieter, more resort-style vacation.
Rent a scooter or hire a taxi?
Scooter for independence, taxi for comfort. We can arrange both.
Is Goa safe for solo women?
Generally yes with standard precautions. Stick to well-lit beaches after dark.
Best New Year in Goa?
Book 90+ days ahead. Beach parties at Curlies, Shiva Valley, Cape Town, Chronicle. Family NYE at Taj Fort Aguada or Leela Cavelossim.
